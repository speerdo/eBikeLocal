🔴 Still Broken — Needs Fixing

1. Homepage H1 is still wrong.
   "Find the Best eBike Local Near You" is still the H1. This was flagged as the #1 priority fix in the last round. Nothing has changed here. It needs to be "Find eBike Shops Near You" or similar — a phrase people actually search.
2. "eBike Buying Resources" section on city pages is missing Denver, Steamboat Springs, and Aspen — but present on Denver.
   Actually Denver has it, which is great. Spot-checking: need to verify the Buying Resources block is present on all city pages, not just Denver. It may have been added selectively. Worth a quick audit on a few smaller cities.
3. Denver city page still has duplicate shops — eBikes USA and eBikes USA Service Center.
   Both are at 1205 W Byers Pl Denver, CO — one with a full Google Places record (4.8 stars, 493 reviews, carrying Aventon/Specialized/Velotric) and one with minimal data (4.6 stars, 30 reviews). These are the same location — a sales floor and a service desk at the same address. The dedup logic didn't catch them because the names differ. Keep the main sales location, remove the service-only record or merge it.
4. Denver city page also has "Epic Cycles Denver" (4.7 stars, 209 reviews) and "Epic Cycles" (no rating, Specialized dealer) as two separate listings. Same address pattern — 5665 Beeler St #100 vs 5665 Beeler Street, Unit 100. Still slipping through the dedup. The street suffix normalization (St → Street) and unit notation normalization (#100 → Unit 100) need to be added to the dedup pipeline.
5. "Basalt Bike & Ski" appears in Denver (2401 15 St Ste 100) but Basalt, CO is 2.5 hours away. This is a data error — the Basalt Bike & Ski shop is based in Basalt, CO, not Denver. The address looks like a brand locator record with a Denver mailing address for a dealer that operates out of Basalt. This type of record should be assigned to the city matching its physical location, not a mailing/billing address.
6. "Denver Fit Loft & Tribella" and "Tribella" are the same shop at the same address (1060 Bannock St). Another dedup miss — different names, same address.
7. Campus Cycles brand badge shows "Trek authorized_online" — there's an underscore with raw data leaking through. The badge label should be "Trek Authorized" or just display the brand name. The authorized_online looks like an internal tier value from the data source that's being displayed directly rather than mapped to a human-readable label.

🟡 SEO Issues Still Outstanding 8. Homepage title tag is fine but H1 is misaligned with it.
The page <title> is "eBikeLocal — Find eBike Shops, Brands & Bikes Near You" but the H1 is still "Find the Best eBike Local Near You". Google weighs both — having them say substantially different things (one mentioning "shops, brands & bikes," the other saying "eBike local") is a weak signal. They should be coherent. 9. "Nearby Cities" on the Denver page still sorted by shop count, not geography.
Boulder (25), Fort Collins (20), Colorado Springs (19), Golden (14), Steamboat Springs (12), Durango (11), Grand Junction (10), Longmont (10). Grand Junction is 4 hours from Denver. Steamboat Springs is 3 hours. Durango is 6 hours. Fort Collins is 1 hour and legitimate. This was flagged last round — the haversine geo-sort for nearby cities still isn't in. 10. State page intro copy is templated but identical in structure across states.
The Colorado intro reads: "Colorado has 379 electric bike shops spanning 60 cities. Specialized, Trek, and Rad Power Bikes are among the most widely carried brands across Colorado dealers. Browse by city below or use the brand chips on city pages to filter shops by what you want to test-ride." Compare this to California last time: same three-sentence pattern, just different numbers. Google's duplicate content detection looks at structural similarity, not just verbatim text. The template needs at least one state-specific sentence — for Colorado that could reference the mountain biking culture, the rebate program, or the Front Range/mountain town split. 11. City page intro copy has a glitch in Denver's.
"Denver dealers carry Specialized, Giant, Trek , and 3 more brands." — there's an extra space before the comma after "Trek". Minor but visible. 12. Brand pages still lack an editorial description/intro beyond the one-paragraph blurb.
The Trek brand page goes: logo → one paragraph → stats → affiliate disclosure → Popular Bikes. There's no section explaining Trek's eBike lineup positioning, what differentiates their eBike range, who their bikes are for, or why someone might choose Trek over Specialized. For a query like "Trek electric bike review" or "is Trek a good eBike brand," this page provides almost nothing beyond a model list. Even 150 words of editorial context would significantly strengthen the page for those informational queries.

🟠 New Issues Found This Pass 13. The "How to Choose an eBike" guide mentions "Rad Power RadRover 6 Plus" and "Rad Power RadWagon 4" as recommended picks. Given Rad's Chapter 11 filing and acquisition by Life EV with their dealer network in transition, recommending these models as purchasing targets (without any caveat) is misleading and could hurt trust. Add a note like the one on the brand page — something like (Note: Rad Power Bikes filed Chapter 11 in December 2025. Verify dealer and warranty status before purchasing.) near any Rad recommendation. 14. The bike detail page for Trek FX+ 1 Midstep shows "Related eBikes" that are all Aventon models. The related bikes are: Himiway C1 Kids eBike, Aventon Soltera.2, Aventon Level 2, Aventon Pace 500.3. None of these are Trek models. For a $1,999 Trek commuter, the related bikes section should show other bikes in the same category and price range — Trek Verve+ 2, Specialized Turbo Vado 3.0, Giant Explore E+ — not budget Aventon models. The related bikes algorithm appears to be pulling the cheapest bikes in the catalog rather than category/price-similar bikes. 15. The shop page description for Campus Cycles reads: "Bike shop boasting range of road or mountain cycles & gear plus repairs in a vintage brick building." This text is clearly pulled verbatim from Google Places/Maps and is written from a third-person reviewer perspective, not a shop description. It reads awkwardly and uses "boasting" which sounds like a review snippet, not a directory description. For shops where the Places description is clearly a review excerpt rather than a business description, the template fallback should generate a cleaner description rather than using the Places text. 16.
